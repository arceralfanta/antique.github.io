About Laua-an

         Laua-an is a municipality located in the province of Antique, Philippines. It is situated in the northern part of the province and is known for its natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. The town has a population of around 20,000 people. The economy of the area is mainly driven by agriculture, fishing and tourism.

         Laua-an is known for its hot springs, waterfalls, and rivers. The most popular attraction is the Biliran Spring, which is a natural hot spring that is believed to have healing properties. Visitors can also explore the nearby waterfalls like the Kanlaon and the Tingib, which are popular spots for swimming and picnics.

         The town of Laua-an is also known for its traditional healing practices, such as the "hilot massage" which is a traditional Filipino massage technique. The town also offers eco-adventure tourism, like the Canopy Walk, which is a 150-meter-long hanging bridge that takes visitors through the lush forest canopy. Laua-an also has many festivals throughout the year, the most popular is the "Laua-an Festival" which is celebrated annually to showcase the town's rich cultural heritage.

BY AIR

         Getting to Laua-an, Antique by air is a bit challenging as the nearest major airport is the Godofredo P. Ramos Airport (also known as Caticlan Airport) which is located in the municipality of Malay, Aklan. From there, one can take a bus or a van bound for the town of Laua-an, but the trip by land can take around 4-5 hours.

BY LAND

         To get to Laua-an by land, one can take a bus or van from major cities in the Visayas region like Iloilo City or Kalibo City bound for the town of Laua-an. The trip can take around 6-7 hours depending on the traffic. From the bus terminal or the van terminal, you can take a tricycle or jeepney to reach the destination.
