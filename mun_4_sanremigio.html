About San Remigio

         Tigbacay is a settlement located about a kilometer from the Malandog River in the Philippines. It was founded by a warrior and hunter named Juan Arangote and his family. Over time, more settlers came and it was established as a barrio in 1797 and became part of the Municipality of Sibalom, with San Vicente de Ferrer as its patron saint.

         On July 1, 1864, Tigbacay was elevated to a municipality and was renamed San Remigio after a Spanish officer Remigio Multo. The town was divided into two barrios called Baladjay and Vilvar in honor of Agustin Baladjay and Basilio Vilvar who worked to separate it from the town of Sibalom.

         The municipality of San Remigio located on the alluvial valley of big Sibalom River, had a long and losing battle against the relentless flood of the river that caused destruction to infrastructures and private property. Thus, the Poblacion was transferred to Calag-itan in 1931 through Executive Order No. 74, series of 1919. The former site, officially the barrios of Baladjay and Vilvar, is now referred to as the "Old Town".

BY AIR

         The newly relaunched airport is located in the capital town of San Jose de Buenavista. Philippine Airlines flies to Antique thrice a week — Tuesday, Thursday, and Sunday. Upon reaching the province, there are jeepneys and tricycles that provide public transportation.

BY LAND

         Buses leave Iloilo City on an hourly basis for the 2 1/2 hours trip to San Jose de Buenavista. From San Jose de Buenavista, ride a jeepney bound for Sibalom. Upon reaching Sibalom, ride another jeepney to San Remigio.
